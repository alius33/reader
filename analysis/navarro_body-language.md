# What Every Body Is Saying — Joe Navarro

## Classification & Context
**Book type:** Manual/how-to — systematic guide to reading nonverbal behaviour, organised body-part by body-part from feet to face.
**Audience:** General readers wanting to read people better in business, relationships, and daily life.
**Author:** Joe Navarro, 25-year FBI Special Agent specialising in counterintelligence and nonverbal behaviour. Learned to read body language as a Cuban immigrant child who couldn't speak English.

## Central Thesis
All nonverbal behaviour is governed by the limbic brain — the "honest brain" that reacts reflexively and without conscious control. The limbic system produces three survival responses (freeze, flight, fight) and pacifying behaviours that follow stress. These are readable, reliable, and universal. The neocortex (thinking brain) can lie; the limbic brain cannot. By learning the limbic system's language — expressed through feet, legs, torso, arms, hands, and face — you can decode people's true thoughts, feelings, and intentions.

## Key Frameworks
- **Triune Brain:** Reptilian (stem), Limbic (mammalian, honest), Neocortex (human, deceptive)
- **Freeze/Flight/Fight:** The three limbic responses to threat, in that order (NOT fight-or-flight)
- **Comfort vs Discomfort:** The master binary for reading all nonverbal behaviour
- **Pacifying Behaviours:** Self-soothing actions that follow stress (neck touching, face rubbing, leg cleansing)
- **Gravity-Defying Behaviours:** Upward movements = positive emotions (bouncy walk, raised arms, arched eyebrows)
- **Ten Commandments of Observation:** Baseline, context, clusters, changes, universal vs idiosyncratic

## Key Principles (12)
1. The limbic brain is the honest brain; the neocortex lies
2. Freeze is the FIRST response (not fight) — movement attracts predators
3. Feet are the most honest body part — furthest from the brain, least controlled
4. Happy feet = genuine excitement; frozen feet = distress
5. Feet point toward what we want and away from what we don't
6. Ventral fronting (exposing torso) = comfort; ventral denial (turning away) = discomfort
7. Gravity-defying gestures signal positive emotions; gravity-succumbing gestures signal negative ones
8. Steepling (fingertip-to-fingertip) is the single most powerful confidence display
9. Lip compression is a universal sign of distress — the lips literally disappear
10. Real smiles involve the eyes (orbicularis oculi); fake smiles are mouth-only
11. There is NO single reliable indicator of deception — look for clusters of discomfort + pacifying
12. Baseline first, then watch for deviations — changes matter more than static positions
