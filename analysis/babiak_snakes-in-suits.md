# Snakes in Suits — Paul Babiak & Robert D. Hare

## Classification & Context
**Book type:** Argument with case studies and a fictional narrative thread ("Dave" the corporate psychopath). Babiak and Hare combine industrial/organisational psychology with Hare's decades of psychopathy research to explain how psychopaths infiltrate and ascend in corporate organisations.
**Author:** Paul Babiak is an industrial and organisational psychologist who consults on executive assessment. Robert Hare is the world's foremost researcher on psychopathy and the creator of the Psychopathy Checklist-Revised (PCL-R), the gold standard diagnostic tool.

## Central Thesis
Corporate environments don't merely tolerate psychopaths — they actively select for them. The traits that define psychopathy (superficial charm, fearless dominance, grandiosity, manipulation, lack of empathy, and willingness to deceive) are routinely mistaken for leadership qualities (charisma, decisiveness, confidence, strategic thinking, and toughness). Psychopaths exploit this confusion to climb organisations, building networks of pawns and patrons while discarding anyone who sees through them. By the time the damage is visible, they're often in positions of significant power.

## Key Frameworks
- The Corporate Psychopath's Playbook: Assessment → Manipulation → Confrontation → Ascension
- Pawns and Patrons: psychopaths sort people into useful (patrons, pawns) and expendable categories
- The Psychopath's Mask: a carefully constructed persona tailored to each audience
- Hare's Psychopathy Checklist traits mapped to corporate "leadership qualities"
- The Organization as Hunting Ground: flat structures, rapid change, and weak HR create ideal conditions

## Best Stories
1. "Dave" — a composite narrative running through the book of a psychopath's rise from interview to executive suite
2. The executive who charmed the board while systematically destroying the careers of everyone who challenged him
3. The job interview as psychopath audition — how they read interviewers and construct the perfect persona in real time
4. Companies that promoted psychopaths because their "bold vision" looked like leadership during turbulent times
