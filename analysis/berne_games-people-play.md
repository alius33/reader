# Games People Play — Eric Berne
## Central Thesis
Human social interactions follow hidden patterns called "games" — repetitive sequences of transactions with a concealed motive and a predictable payoff (usually negative). Berne's Transactional Analysis identifies three ego states (Parent, Adult, Child) that govern all communication. Games are played when communication crosses ego states in deceptive ways. Common games include "Why Don't You—Yes But," "If It Weren't For You," "Now I've Got You," and "Ain't It Awful." Recognition of these games is the first step to autonomy.
## Key Frameworks: Three Ego States (Parent, Adult, Child), Transactions (complementary, crossed, ulterior), Games (with formula: Con + Gimmick → Response → Switch → Crossup → Payoff), Life Scripts, Strokes (units of recognition)
