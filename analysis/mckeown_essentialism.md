# Essentialism — Greg McKeown
## Central Thesis
If you don't prioritise your life, someone else will. Most people try to do everything for everyone and spread themselves too thin. The Essentialist does fewer things, better. It's not about getting more done — it's about getting the right things done. The way of the Essentialist is a systematic discipline for discerning what is absolutely essential, then eliminating everything that is not.
## Key Frameworks
- Nonessentialist vs Essentialist (comparison across every dimension)
- Explore → Eliminate → Execute (the three-phase Essentialist process)
- The 90% Rule (if it's not a clear 90% yes, it's a no)
- Trade-offs are not negative — they are the essence of strategy
- "Less but better" as a life operating system
## Best Stories: Dieter Rams ("Less but better"), Southwest Airlines (said no to meals/hub-and-spoke to be the best low-cost airline), CEO who said yes to everything and failed vs one who said no to everything and thrived
