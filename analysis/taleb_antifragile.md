# Antifragile — Nassim Nicholas Taleb

## Classification & Context
**Book type:** Argument — philosophical treatise on a new concept (antifragility) applied across domains.
**Author:** Nassim Nicholas Taleb is a former options trader, risk analyst, and Distinguished Professor of Risk Engineering at NYU. Author of The Black Swan, Fooled by Randomness, and Skin in the Game.

## Central Thesis
We have no word for the opposite of fragile. "Robust" is not it — robust things merely resist shocks. The true opposite of fragile is "antifragile" — things that actually gain from disorder, volatility, stressors, and uncertainty. Some systems need stress to improve: muscles, immune systems, economies, careers. The modern world systematically suppresses volatility, removes stressors, and creates fragility — making catastrophic blowups inevitable. The solution is to structure your life, portfolio, career, and institutions to be antifragile.

## Key Frameworks
- The Triad: Fragile / Robust / Antifragile
- Via Negativa: improvement through removal, not addition
- Barbell Strategy: combine extreme safety with extreme risk, avoid the middle
- Skin in the Game: those who take risks should bear the consequences
- Iatrogenics: harm done by the healer (intervention that makes things worse)
- Lindy Effect: for non-perishable things, expected future life = current age
- Optionality: having options is antifragile; being locked in is fragile
- Green Lumber Fallacy: you don't need to understand the theory to profit from the practice

## Best Stories
1. Damocles, Phoenix, Hydra — the Triad explained through mythology
2. Fat Tony vs Dr. John — the street-smart trader beats the PhD because he has skin in the game
3. The Soviet-Harvard illusion — top-down planning fails; bottom-up tinkering works
4. Grandmothers vs the FDA — grandma's remedies survived centuries (Lindy); new drugs haven't been tested
5. The Green Lumber story — a trader made a fortune in green lumber without knowing it meant freshly cut (not painted green)
