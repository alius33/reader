# Crucial Conversations — Kerry Patterson et al.
## Central Thesis
A crucial conversation has three characteristics: opposing opinions, strong emotions, and high stakes. Most people handle these badly — they either avoid them entirely or handle them with silence or violence. The key skill is maintaining "dialogue" — the free flow of meaning between people — especially when it's hardest. The tools: Start with Heart (clarify what you really want), Learn to Look (spot when safety is at risk), Make It Safe (restore safety through mutual purpose and mutual respect), STATE your path, and Explore Others' Paths.
## Key Frameworks: Crucial Conversation definition (high stakes + strong emotions + opposing opinions), The Pool of Shared Meaning, Silence vs Violence, STATE (Share facts, Tell your story, Ask for others' paths, Talk tentatively, Encourage testing), Mutual Purpose + Mutual Respect, Move to Action (who does what by when)
