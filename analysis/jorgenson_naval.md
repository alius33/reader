# Analysis: The Almanack of Naval Ravikant — Eric Jorgenson

## Structure
Curated compilation of Naval Ravikant's tweets, podcast transcripts, and interviews. Two major parts: **Part I — Wealth** (how to get rich without getting lucky) and **Part II — Happiness** (how to be happy without external conditions). Not a traditional narrative — reads more like a wisdom anthology.

## Core Arguments

### Wealth
- Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep — not salary, not status
- Three ingredients: **specific knowledge** (can't be trained for, found by following genuine curiosity), **accountability** (take risks under your own name), **leverage** (code, media, capital, labour)
- Code and media are "permissionless leverage" — you don't need anyone's permission to write code or create content
- Judgment is the most important skill — leverage multiplied by good judgment creates wealth

### Happiness
- Happiness is a **skill**, not a reward — it's the default state when you stop desiring
- "The Backwards Law": the desire for a more positive experience is itself a negative experience
- Key practices: meditation, present-moment awareness, desire reduction, choosing to interpret events neutrally
- "Every desire is a chosen unhappiness"

## Strengths
- Extreme compression: Naval says in one sentence what others take chapters to say
- Practical frameworks (specific knowledge, leverage, accountability) that are immediately actionable
- Rare combination of wealth creation and philosophical happiness in one framework
- Free book — consistent with Naval's ethos of permissionless leverage

## Weaknesses
- No narrative arc — curated tweets and transcripts lack flow
- Survivorship bias — Naval's advice works for Naval but may not generalize
- Happiness section is thinner and more derivative (Buddhism, Stoicism) than the wealth section
- Some contradictions between "be patient" and "act urgently" not fully resolved

## Key Frameworks
1. **Specific Knowledge + Accountability + Leverage = Wealth**
2. **Happiness = Reality − Expectations**
3. **Permissionless leverage** (code, media) vs permission-based leverage (capital, labour)
4. **Judgment** as the highest-leverage skill

## Quotable Lines
- "Seek wealth, not money or status."
- "You're not going to get rich renting out your time."
- "The most important skill for getting rich is becoming a perpetual learner."
- "A calm mind, a fit body, a house full of love. These things cannot be bought."
- "Happiness is what's there when you remove the sense that something is missing."

## Connections
- Extends Peter Thiel's "definite optimism" from Zero to One
- Happiness framework aligns with Marcus Aurelius's Meditations
- Wealth framework complements Morgan Housel's behavioural approach in The Psychology of Money
- Leverage concept maps to Robert Greene's power dynamics
