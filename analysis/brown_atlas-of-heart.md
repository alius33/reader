# Atlas of the Heart — Brene Brown
## Central Thesis
We experience approximately 87 emotions and experiences, but most people can only identify three: happy, sad, and angry. This emotional illiteracy is not a personality flaw — it is a skills gap that costs us our relationships, our wellbeing, and our ability to connect. By building a shared vocabulary for what we feel, we develop what Brown calls "grounded confidence" — the ability to navigate emotional complexity without armoring up or shutting down. Language is the portal to meaning, and meaning is the portal to connection.
## Book Type: Taxonomy — 87 emotions and experiences organised into 13 thematic chapters (Places We Go)
## Key Frameworks:
- **Emotional Granularity** — the ability to make fine-grained distinctions between similar emotions (e.g., envy vs. jealousy, sympathy vs. empathy vs. compassion)
- **Near Enemies** — emotions that look similar on the surface but differ fundamentally (pity disguised as compassion, detachment disguised as equanimity)
- **Grounded Confidence** — the opposite of armoured certainty; comes from emotional vocabulary, not from having all the answers
- **The Anatomy of Trust (BRAVING)** — Boundaries, Reliability, Accountability, Vault, Integrity, Non-judgement, Generosity
- **Comparative Suffering** — the belief that you shouldn't feel pain because someone else has it worse; Brown argues this never leads to empathy
- **Cognitive Empatny / Affective Empathy / Empathic Concern** — three distinct types of empathy with different mechanisms
## Chapter Structure (13 Chapters):
1. Places We Go When Things Are Uncertain or Too Much — stress, overwhelm, anxiety, worry, avoidance, excitement, dread
2. Places We Go When We Compare — comparison, admiration, reverence, envy, jealousy, resentment, schadenfreude, freudenfreude
3. Places We Go When Things Don't Go as Planned — boredom, disappointment, expectations, regret, discouragement, resignation, frustration
4. Places We Go When It's Beyond Us — awe, wonder, confusion, curiosity, interest, surprise
5. Places We Go When Things Aren't What They Seem — amusement, bittersweetness, nostalgia, cognitive dissonance, paradox, irony, sarcasm
6. Places We Go When We're Hurting — anguish, hopelessness, despair, sadness, grief
7. Places We Go With Others — compassion, pity, empathy, sympathy, boundaries, comparative suffering
8. Places We Go When We Fall Short — shame, self-compassion, perfectionism, guilt, humiliation, embarrassment
9. Places We Go When We Search for Connection — belonging, fitting in, connection, disconnection, insecurity, invisibility, loneliness
10. Places We Go When the Heart Is Open — love, lovelessness, heartbreak, trust, self-trust, betrayal, defensiveness, flooding, hurt
11. Places We Go to Self-Assess — pride, hubris, humility
12. Places We Go When Life is Good — joy, happiness, calm, contentment, gratitude, foreboding joy, relief, tranquility
13. Places We Go When We Feel Wronged — anger, contempt, disgust, dehumanization, hate, self-righteousness

## Key Distinctions (Core Teaching Method):
- Envy vs. Jealousy — envy is wanting what someone has; jealousy is fearing you'll lose what you have
- Sympathy vs. Empathy — sympathy is "I feel for you"; empathy is "I feel with you"
- Empathy vs. Compassion — empathy is feeling with; compassion is feeling with plus acting
- Guilt vs. Shame — guilt is "I did something bad"; shame is "I am bad"
- Fitting In vs. Belonging — fitting in requires you to change; belonging requires you to be yourself
- Happiness vs. Joy — happiness is tied to circumstances; joy is tied to connection and meaning
- Pride vs. Hubris — pride is earned and grounded; hubris is inflated and fragile
- Pity vs. Compassion — pity creates distance; compassion creates connection
