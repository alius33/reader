# In Sheep's Clothing — George K. Simon

## Classification & Context
**Book type:** Argument-driven clinical guide. Simon builds a taxonomy of covert-aggressive personality types and their manipulation tactics, then provides a defence framework. Written in accessible clinical language for a general audience.
**Author:** Dr. George K. Simon is a clinical psychologist specialising in manipulative personalities and character disturbance. He challenges the mainstream therapeutic assumption that all problematic behaviour stems from insecurity or anxiety — arguing that some people behave aggressively because it works.

## Central Thesis
Not all aggression is overt. The most dangerous manipulators are covert-aggressives — people who pursue what they want through hidden, underhanded tactics while maintaining a surface appearance of reasonableness, charm, or victimhood. Traditional psychology often fails to identify them because it assumes everyone is motivated by fear or insecurity. Simon argues that covert-aggressives know exactly what they're doing — they fight for dominance while disguising the fight. Recognising their tactics is the first and most important step in defending yourself.

## Key Frameworks
- Two types of aggression: overt (openly hostile) and covert (disguised as something benign)
- Covert-aggressive personality: not neurotic, not insecure — actively and deliberately manipulative
- Tactics taxonomy: minimisation, denial, rationalisation, diversion, lying by omission, covert intimidation, guilt-tripping, shaming, playing the victim, vilifying the victim, playing the servant role, seduction, projecting blame
- Defence framework: trust your gut instinct, judge actions not words, set clear boundaries, avoid JADE (Justify, Argue, Defend, Explain)

## Best Stories
1. Clinical case of "Amanda" — a woman who couldn't figure out why she always felt manipulated by her seemingly loving partner
2. The employee who used "I was just trying to help" as a weapon to undermine colleagues
3. The parent who guilt-tripped adult children while framing it as concern
