# Why Trump Will Win

## Core Question

Why will Trump win the 2024 presidential election, and what does his choice of vice president reveal about the likelihood of war with Iran?

Prof. Jiang frames this as a two-part question:
1. **Electoral analysis:** Why is Biden's 2020 coalition falling apart, and what strategic move can Trump make to seal victory?
2. **Foreign policy implication:** If Nikki Haley becomes VP, she will be the person in the White House agitating for war against Iran — connecting this electoral analysis directly to the series' central question about the coming war.

Secondary question raised at the end: How do we build and test analytical models about the world? Prof. Jiang uses this lecture as a case study in predictive reasoning — make a testable prediction, observe reality, refine the model.

## Lecture Type

Geopolitical analysis — electoral-strategic argument. The lecture analyses the composition of Biden's 2020 coalition (black voters, young people, college-educated, women, suburban voters), explains why each group is less motivated in 2024, identifies the suburban vote as the decisive swing factor, and argues that Trump can win by picking Nikki Haley as VP — replicating Biden's 2020 Kamala Harris strategy. The lecture then connects Haley to anti-Iran forces (United Against a Nuclear Iran, Boeing, Christians United for Israel, Sheldon Adelson), arguing that her VP role would be to push for war with Iran.

## Central Thesis

Prof. Jiang argues that Trump will win the 2024 presidential election because Biden's 2020 coalition is collapsing across every demographic group — black voters are disillusioned by inflation and immigration, young people are protesting Gaza, college-educated voters see weak leadership, and the crucial suburban vote is up for grabs. The suburbs decide American elections: Trump won them in 2016 (defeating Clinton), lost them in 2020 (Biden flipped 2 million voters), and can win them back by picking Nikki Haley as VP. The Biden-Harris model proved that picking a former rival signals growth, empathy, and magnanimity — qualities the suburbs prize. Trump-Haley would replicate this exactly: two public enemies who reconcile, signalling Trump has "changed." But Haley's financial ties — to United Against a Nuclear Iran (funded by Sheldon Adelson/Netanyahu's patron), Boeing (weapons manufacturer), and Christians United for Israel (evangelical war advocates) — reveal that her VP role would be to push for war with Iran. This connects the electoral analysis to the series' driving question: Trump's victory, combined with Haley's hawkish connections, makes war with Iran highly probable.

Chain of reasoning: Biden coalition collapsing → suburbs are the decisive swing → Biden won suburbs via Harris redemption narrative → Trump can copy with Haley → Haley's financial backers are all anti-Iran forces → Trump + Haley = war with Iran.

## Argument Structure

### Argument 1: Biden's 2020 Coalition Is Falling Apart Across Every Demographic

**The claim:** Biden won in 2020 by assembling an unusually broad coalition driven by anti-Trump sentiment (George Floyd protests) and pandemic fear. In 2024, every element of this coalition is weaker because Biden's record has disappointed each group.

**Evidence for:**

*Black voters (91% Biden in 2020):*
- Loyalty to Obama's VP was a one-time debt — now "the debt is paid off"
- George Floyd protests galvanised anti-Trump sentiment; no equivalent event in 2024
- Inflation has disproportionately hurt poor people, especially black Americans — "standard of living goes way down"
- Illegal immigration doubled from 10 million to 20 million under Biden — immigrants take jobs from poor people and push wages down
- $10s of billions sent to Ukraine while black Americans' living standards decline — perceived as bad priorities
- Polls show Biden's lead with black voters declining

*Young people:*
- In 2020, they were galvanised by George Floyd protests — saw Trump as a "hateful racist they must remove"
- In 2024, Gaza protests on college campuses. Young people won't vote for Trump, but many will sit out the election as a protest against Biden's Israel policy.

*College-educated (70% for Biden in 2020):*
- Still core Democratic base — will vote Democratic
- But "less enthusiastic" due to perceived poor global leadership: Ukraine, Gaza, Biden appears "too old, too weak, too feeble to stand against Putin"
- Will vote "primarily against Trump" rather than for Biden — lower motivation

*Women (54% Biden in 2020):*
- Will still vote Democratic, especially driven by abortion rights
- This is the one group Prof. Jiang says will remain consistent

*Overall pattern:* Every group that powered Biden's 2020 victory is either switching (black voters), abstaining (young people), or less enthusiastic (college-educated). Only women remain consistent but not sufficient alone.

**Evidence against:** None presented. Prof. Jiang does note that the margin in 2020 was only 65,000 votes despite the 81M vs. 74M headline — suggesting even small shifts could flip the result.

**Verdict:** Prof. Jiang treats this as straightforward — the coalition cannot hold because the conditions that created it (George Floyd, pandemic) no longer exist, and Biden's record (inflation, immigration, Ukraine, Gaza) has alienated each group.

### Argument 2: The Suburban Vote Decides American Elections

**The claim:** The suburbs — between urban (Democratic) and rural (Republican) — are where the swing voters live. Winning or losing the suburbs determines the election outcome.

**Evidence for:**
- 2016: Only 45% of suburbs voted for Clinton → Trump won
- 2020: 54% of suburbs voted for Biden (a flip of 2 million voters) → Biden won
- Urban areas "overwhelmingly support the Democratic Party"; rural areas "overwhelmingly support the Republican Party" — neither swings
- The only variable is the suburbs

**Evidence against:** None presented.

**Verdict:** Prof. Jiang treats this as an established principle of American electoral analysis: "for Trump to win this year, he needs to win the suburban vote."

### Argument 3: Biden Won the Suburbs in 2020 Through Three Specific Mechanisms (Media Narrative, Pandemic, Kamala Harris)

**Evidence for:**

*Mechanism 3: Kamala Harris — the decisive factor*

## Key Concepts & Frameworks

### The Electoral College and the 65,000-Vote Margin
- **Definition:** America's indirect election system where the popular vote does not directly determine the winner; instead, state-by-state results and the allocation of electoral votes decide the presidency
- **How it works:** Despite Biden winning the popular vote 81M to 74M (appearing lopsided), only 65,000 votes in key battleground states separated the candidates — "if 65,000 people in key battleground states flip their votes, then Trump would have won"
- **What it explains:** Why coalition analysis matters more than raw vote totals; why small shifts in specific demographics can flip an election

### The Biden Coalition (Five Components)
- **Definition:** The five demographic groups whose alignment produced Biden's 2020 victory
- **Components:**
  1. Black voters (91% Biden) — loyalty to Obama's VP + George Floyd galvanisation
  2. Young people — anti-Trump energy from George Floyd
  3. College-educated (70% Biden) — coastal professional-managerial elite, core Democratic base
  4. Women (54% Biden) — anti-Trump + abortion rights
  5. Suburban voters (54% Biden, flipped from 45% Clinton in 2016) — the decisive swing group
- **Why it's collapsing in 2024:** Each group's motivation was event-specific (George Floyd, pandemic) and Biden's record has disappointed each in different ways (inflation, immigration, Gaza, perceived weakness)

### The Suburban Swing as Electoral Determinant
- **Definition:** The principle that suburban voters — between solidly Democratic urban areas and solidly Republican rural areas — decide American presidential elections
- **Evidence:** 2016 (45% suburbs → Clinton lost), 2020 (54% suburbs → Biden won) — the 2-million-voter swing in the suburbs was the election
- **What suburban voters want:** Stability, a unifier, safety, someone who is "conservative" (in the temperament sense), educated, articulate, establishment
- **Strategic implication:** Both parties must tailor their VP pick and messaging to suburban sensibilities

### The Redemption Narrative Strategy (VP Pick as Signal)
- **Definition:** The strategy of picking a former rival as VP to signal personal growth, magnanimity, and empathy to suburban voters
- **How it works:**
  1. Candidate and future VP publicly fight during the primary
  2. Future VP says things that appear to disqualify them from being picked
  3. Candidate picks them anyway, surprising everyone
  4. Media and voters interpret this as: the candidate doesn't hold grudges, listens, is a team player, and has empathy
  5. These qualities are exactly what suburban voters (especially women) prize
- **2020 application:** Biden-Harris. Harris attacked Biden as a racist on national television over school integration. Biden picked her anyway. Suburbs responded.
- **2024 application:** Trump-Haley. Haley said Trump is divisive; Trump called her "a bird brain." If Trump picks her anyway, same effect.
- **Why it works on women especially:** "Women want compassionate, empathetic leadership that is decisive and bold but is also tolerant"
- **Counter-example:** Hillary Clinton lost the suburbs in 2016 despite being a woman because she "didn't come across as compassionate, forgiving, and tolerant"

### Politics as Theatre (WWE Analogy)
- **Definition:** Prof. Jiang's framework that political conflicts are performed for public consumption, not reflective of genuine relationships
- **Source analogy:** Trump's WWE appearances, studied in a previous class — Vince McMahon "challenges Trump to a fight" as staged entertainment
- **Application:** "What Nikki Haley and Donald Trump are doing is pretending to be bitter enemies so that their reconciliation will be so dramatic"
- **Supporting evidence:** Haley and Trump got along well during her 2016-2018 tenure as UN ambassador; Trump considered her for VP in 2020
- **Broader principle:** "Very successful politicians are first and foremost opportunists. They have no ideas, they have no principles. All they care about is political power." Same applies to rich people (money) and celebrities (fame). All will "sell their own mother" for their primary currency.

### United Against a Nuclear Iran (Anti-Iran Network Hub)
- **Definition:** An organisation whose "primary purpose is to create conflict between the United States and Iran"
- **Activities:** Targets companies and individuals who support or do business with Iran
- **Key donor:** Sheldon Adelson — pro-Israel billionaire, casino magnate, primary patron and financial donor to Benjamin Netanyahu
- **Connection to Haley:** Hired Haley after she left office, paid her substantial sums — primary source of her post-government wealth
- **What it explains:** The mechanism through which pro-Israel, anti-Iran money flows into American politics and positions sympathetic politicians for power

### Analytical Models and Predictive Testing
- **Definition:** Prof. Jiang's methodological framework for understanding geopolitics — build a model, make testable predictions, observe reality, refine the model
- **Application to this lecture:** The prediction that Trump wins and picks Haley is explicitly framed as testable. "It's possible in August he does not pick Nikki Haley... it's also possible that in November Trump loses. But that's fine, because if I'm wrong, then I go back to this analysis and make changes so that it's much more reflective and accurate."
- **Purpose:** "That's how we try to think critically about the world — by building analytical models that we can then test against reality and then refine."
- **Alternative scenario acknowledged:** JD Vance as VP pick — "very loyal to Trump" but would not produce the same suburban swing

## Historical Narratives & Examples

### Story 1: The 2020 Election — Biden's Razor-Thin Victory
- **Who:** Joe Biden, Donald Trump, the American electorate
- **When:** November 2020
- **Where:** United States
- **What happened:** The 2020 election produced the highest turnout since 1900 — two out of three American adults voted. Biden won 81 million votes to Trump's 74 million. But because of the electoral college system, the actual margin of victory was only 65,000 votes in key battleground states. If 65,000 people flipped, Trump would have won.
- **Outcome:** Biden won, but the margin reveals extreme vulnerability — small demographic shifts in 2024 could easily flip the result.
- **Which argument it supports:** Argument 1 (every group matters; even small erosion of any part of Biden's coalition could cost him)

### Story 2: George Floyd and the Black Vote Mobilisation
- **Who:** George Floyd (black man), a white police officer, black voters nationwide
- **When:** May 2020
- **Where:** Minneapolis, then nationwide
- **What happened:** George Floyd was strangled to death by a white police officer, captured on video. This caused nationwide riots and protests. Trump "came out and did not acknowledge the pain that black people felt." Black voters were galvanised: 91% voted Biden.
- **Outcome:** The protests both mobilised black voters and terrified suburban voters — a double effect that benefited Biden. But this was a one-time event that cannot be replicated in 2024.
- **Which argument it supports:** Arguments 1 and 3 (explains the temporary nature of Biden's coalition strength)

### Story 3: Biden's Coalition Erosion — Inflation, Immigration, Ukraine
- **Who:** Black Americans, poor Americans, illegal immigrants, the Biden administration
- **When:** 2021-2024
- **Where:** United States
- **What happened:** Under Biden, inflation rose (hurting poor people disproportionately), illegal immigration doubled from 10 million to 20 million (taking jobs from poor people, pushing wages down), and tens of billions of dollars went to Ukraine for "a war that has nothing to do with America." Black voters are asking: "What has Biden done for us these past four years?"
- **Outcome:** Biden's lead with black voters is shrinking in polls. Young people are protesting Gaza instead of supporting Biden. The coalition is fragmenting.
- **Which argument it supports:** Argument 1 (systematic erosion of every demographic)

### Story 4: The June 2019 Democratic Debate — Harris Attacks Biden
- **Who:** Kamala Harris, Joe Biden
- **When:** June 2019
- **Where:** First Democratic primary debate (televised nationally)
- **What happened:** Harris and Biden were both considered heavy favourites for the nomination. On national television, Harris turned to Biden and said: "Joe, you've been in Washington for 50 years, and some of your policies I don't support. You did not support the integration of black and white students. - **Outcome:** "Everyone thought there's no way Biden gets the nomination and picks her as VP." When he did pick her, it became the defining signal of his candidacy: he doesn't hold grudges, he listens, he has empathy. This is what won the suburbs — especially suburban women.
- **Which argument it supports:** Argument 3 (the Harris redemption narrative as the key mechanism) and Argument 4 (the template Trump can replicate)
