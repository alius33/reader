# You Are Not So Smart — David McRaney

## Classification & Context
**Book type:** Taxonomy — 48 short chapters, each covering one cognitive bias, logical fallacy, or heuristic.
**Author:** David McRaney is a journalist and psychology enthusiast who started the popular blog "You Are Not So Smart," which became this book. He later wrote "You Are Now Less Dumb."

## Central Thesis
You are not as rational, logical, or self-aware as you think. Your brain is a flawed machine running on shortcuts, self-deception, and motivated reasoning. Each chapter reveals one way your brain systematically misleads you — from confirmation bias to the Dunning-Kruger effect to the sunk cost fallacy. The format: each chapter opens with "The Misconception" (what you believe) and "The Truth" (what the science shows).

## Key Biases Covered (selection)
Priming, Confabulation, Confirmation Bias, Hindsight Bias, Texas Sharpshooter Fallacy, Procrastination, Normalcy Bias, Introspection Illusion, Availability Heuristic, Bystander Effect, Dunning-Kruger Effect, Sunk Cost Fallacy, Anchoring Effect, Brand Loyalty, Argument from Authority, Groupthink, Affect Heuristic, Self-Serving Bias, Spotlight Effect, Third Person Effect, Learned Helplessness, Embodied Cognition, Catharsis
