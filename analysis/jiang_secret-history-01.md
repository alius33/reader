# How Power Works

## Core Question

How does power work? If we can figure this out, we achieve liberation — we understand how we are being manipulated and become free to see the world as it truly is.

Secondary framing: The course is structured around revealing "the secret history of humanity" — the history taught in schools is false, implanted by powerful people, and understanding power's mechanisms is the key to uncovering what actually happened.

## Lecture Type

Framework/Introduction — This is the opening lecture of the entire Secret History series. Rather than a historical deep dive, Prof. Jiang lays out the conceptual architecture for the entire semester: what the course is about (power), how it will work (geopolitics in the present, predictions for the future, analysis of the past), and the three primary mechanisms of power (money, individualism, the nation state). It is a provocative orientation lecture designed to shatter student assumptions.

## Central Thesis

Power works through alchemy — turning nothing into everything. Three artificial constructs (money, the individual, the nation state) have been implanted into human consciousness through what Prof. Jiang calls brainwashing. These constructs are relatively recent inventions, all traceable to monotheism, and they serve to make people work as hard as possible while believing they are free. The true value extracted is human labour; money is merely the incentive mechanism. Understanding this is the path to liberation.

The entire semester will trace how monotheism created these constructs and thereby created the modern world — and why the ancient polytheistic worldview (with its concept of eudaimonia) was in many ways superior.

## Argument Structure

### Argument 1: Money is an infinite resource; scarcity is manufactured

**The claim:** Banks create money from nothing. Money is infinite. Poverty exists not because of scarcity but because powerful people need poverty to motivate labour.

**Evidence for:**
- Banking thought experiment: $5M deposits + $5M loans = $9.5M (not zero)
- Chinese banks became the world's largest by creating money to finance infrastructure
- Historical banking: gold receipts were duplicated — same gold backing multiple contracts
- Food waste proves abundance — "go to the garbage dump and see how much food is wasted"

**Evidence against (from students):**
- Fractional reserve system means banks can't loan all deposits — Prof. Jiang acknowledges but says the money creation still happens
- Food and land ARE finite resources — Prof. Jiang distinguishes between "infinite" and "abundant enough"

**Verdict:** Prof. Jiang fully endorses this view. Scarcity is a manufactured illusion. Economic crises and wars exist to destroy excess money and maintain the illusion.

### Argument 2: The individual is a modern invention that serves power

**The claim:** Two competing worldviews — polytheism (no individual agency, gods control fate) vs. modern science (individuals control their own lives through synapses and memories). The second worldview appears liberating but actually enslaves.

**Evidence for polytheism being more accurate:**
- Ancient punishment was exile (not death) — proves the individual had no independent existence
- Ancient wealth-sharing through feasting — collective happiness, not individual accumulation
- Polytheism inspired eudaimonia (flourishing) — the Greeks were culturally superior
- Modern worldview makes collective action impossible: "you think all your problems are individual"
- Psychiatry makes people worse, not better — designed for dependency, not cure

**Evidence for modern view (as presented by students):**
- Neuroscience and psychology are "real science"
- We can manage anger, depression through therapy and self-reflection

**Verdict:** Prof. Jiang argues the polytheistic worldview is more accurate. The modern view was created by powerful people because: (1) individual responsibility = easier to control, (2) makes people work harder, (3) prevents collective action = powerlessness.

### Argument 3: School exists to brainwash, not to educate

**The claim:** Compulsory schooling is not about learning — it's about brainwashing students to believe in the nation state.

**Evidence for:**
- Apprenticeship produces better practitioners than formal schooling (doctor thought experiment)
- Only three historical societies had compulsory education: Sparta, Aztecs, Prussia — all war societies
- School separates children from parents → insecurity → receptiveness to authority
- Before nation states, identity was local (village, neighbourhood)
- History is "the false memory of a nation state"

**Evidence against:**
- Students note parents are also brainwashed — Prof. Jiang agrees but says parental presence still provides security that resists brainwashing

**Verdict:** Prof. Jiang fully endorses. School serves the nation state by implanting the concept of national identity.

## Key Concepts & Frameworks

### Paradigm
- Definition: "A story or model or understanding" — the dominant explanation a society accepts without thinking
- Function: The course is about examining paradigms and testing whether they match evidence
- Source: Introduced here, recurs throughout all 28 lectures
- Attributed to Immanuel Kant's philosophical framework

### Kant's Noumena vs. Phenomena
- Noumena: "things in themselves" — objective reality, unknowable to us
- Phenomena: "things that appear to us" — reality as filtered through our senses
- Example: time and space do not exist in reality but are necessary for us to process reality
- Implication: "Reality is what we imagine it to be. Life is a constant act of imagination."

- Historical alchemy: turning lead into gold, dismissed as pseudoscience

## Historical Narratives & Examples

### 1. The Banking Thought Experiment
- Who: Prof. Jiang (as hypothetical banker) and students (as depositors)
- What: $5M deposited, $5M loaned, yet $9.5M exists — not zero
- When: Presented as a general principle
- Argument supported: Money creation from nothing

### 2. Chinese Banks Becoming World's Largest
- Who: Bank of China, ICBC, Agricultural Bank of China
- What: Became the world's largest banks in ~20 years by creating money for infrastructure
- When: Past 20 years (roughly 2000s–2020s)
- Argument supported: Banks create money from nothing; this is how modern economies work

### 3. Origin of Banking Through Gold Receipts
- Who: Italian merchants (implied — merchants who needed to facilitate trade)
- What: Gold deposited → receipts issued → receipts used as money → same gold backing multiple receipts
- When: Medieval/early modern period
- Argument supported: Money creation has always been about turning nothing into everything

### 4. Merchant Cartels and Central Banking
- Who: Banking families who intermarried across Europe
- What: Formed cartels to manage bank run risk; collectively forced repayment from kings
- When: Medieval/early modern Europe
- Argument supported: Central banking originated as a cartel to protect money creation

### 5. Ancient Wealth-Sharing Feasts
- Who: Universal human practice (including Chinese villages)
- What: When someone became wealthy, the first act was to hold a feast for the entire community
- When: "Throughout most of human history"
- Example: Chinese villager goes to Beijing, opens restaurant, makes money, returns to village → big feast
- Argument supported: Individual accumulation is modern; ancient happiness was collective

### 6. Sparta's Compulsory Education
- Who: Sparta, one of thousands of Greek city-states
- What: Children from age 5-6 put in school, beaten by older children — compulsory
- When: ~3000 years ago
- Argument supported: Education originated as war preparation, not learning

### 7. The Aztecs' Compulsory Education
- Who: The Aztec empire in Central America
- What: Free compulsory education for all children; society built around warfare and human sacrifice
- When: Pre-European contact
- Argument supported: Education as war preparation

### 8. Prussia's Education System
- Who: Prussia (German state)
- What: Created the education model the entire world uses today; greatest military in Europe
- When: 18th-19th century
- Argument supported: Modern schooling is fundamentally a Prussian war-preparation system

### 9. The Doctor Thought Experiment
- Who: Hypothetical 12-year-old aspiring doctor
- What: Path 1 (apprenticeship in hospital, 10 years) vs. Path 2 (Harvard undergrad + Harvard Medical School)
- Outcome: Path 1 produces a better doctor by age 30; Path 2 has never worked in a hospital
- Argument supported: School is not about learning; apprenticeship is superior
