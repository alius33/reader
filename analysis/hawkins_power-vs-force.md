# Power vs. Force -- David R. Hawkins

## Classification and Context

**Book type:** Argument -- a single thesis (there is a measurable hierarchy of consciousness, and true power operates through alignment with higher levels rather than through force) supported by kinesiology research, historical examples, and spiritual philosophy.

**Audience:** Readers interested in consciousness, spirituality, personal development, and the intersection of science and metaphysics.

**Author:** David R. Hawkins, M.D., Ph.D., was a psychiatrist, physician, researcher, and spiritual teacher. He ran one of the largest psychiatric practices in the United States (1,000+ patients per year, 50 therapists, 25 offices). Co-authored Orthomolecular Psychiatry with Nobel laureate Linus Pauling in 1973.

## Central Thesis

All human behaviour is governed by energy fields that can be measured and calibrated on a logarithmic scale from 1 to 1,000. Below the critical threshold of 200 (Courage), consciousness operates through force. Above 200, consciousness operates through power. The body's muscle-testing response (applied kinesiology) provides a direct yes/no tool for distinguishing truth from falsehood. True power needs no defence, creates no enemies, and endures; force always creates counter-force, requires constant energy, and ultimately collapses.

## Key Frameworks

- **Map of Consciousness (1-1000):** Logarithmic scale from Shame (20) through Enlightenment (700-1000)
- **Power vs. Force dichotomy:** Power = life, truth, levels above 200; Force = falsehood, coercion, levels below 200
- **Applied Kinesiology:** Binary yes/no muscle response to truth vs. falsehood
- **Attractor Fields:** From chaos theory -- consciousness levels act as attractors organising behaviour
- **Critical Level 200:** Dividing line between destructive and constructive energy fields
