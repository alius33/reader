# Zero to One — Peter Thiel
## Central Thesis
Going from 0 to 1 (creating something new) is fundamentally different from going from 1 to n (copying what works). Progress comes from monopoly, not competition. The best businesses are monopolies that are so good at what they do that no one else can offer a close substitute. Competition destroys profits. Contrarian thinking — believing something true that most people disagree with — is the foundation of every great company.
## Key Frameworks: 0 to 1 vs 1 to n, Contrarian Question ("What important truth do few people agree with you on?"), Monopoly vs Competition, Power Law (a few ventures produce all the returns), Definite vs Indefinite Optimism/Pessimism (2x2), Seven Questions Every Business Must Answer, Secrets (what important truths are not yet widely known?)
## Best Stories: PayPal Mafia, Cleantech bubble failures, Palantir's contrarian approach, Facebook as a monopoly disguised as a competitor
## Cross-References: Antifragile - Taleb (optionality and barbell), The Lean Startup - Ries (Thiel disagrees with lean/iterative approach), The Effective Executive - Drucker (concentration on what matters)
