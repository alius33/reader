# Analysis: Wooden on Leadership — John Wooden & Steve Jamison

## Book Metadata
- **Title:** Wooden on Leadership: How to Create a Winning Organization
- **Authors:** John Wooden with Steve Jamison
- **Year:** 2005
- **Publisher:** McGraw-Hill
- **Type:** Non-taxonomy leadership book (15 chapters + prologue + epilogue + notebook section)

## Structure

### Part 1: The Foundation for My Leadership (Chapters 1-3)
- Introduction: Wooden's definition of success, father's influence, competing against yourself
- Chapter 1: The Pyramid of Success — Foundation tier (Industriousness, Enthusiasm, Friendship, Loyalty, Cooperation)
- Chapter 2: Second Tier (Self-Control, Alertness, Initiative, Intentness)
- Chapter 3: Heart of the Pyramid (Condition, Skill, Team Spirit) + near-top (Poise, Confidence) + apex (Competitive Greatness) + mortar (Faith, Patience)

### Part 2: Lessons in Leadership (Chapters 4-15)
- Chapter 4: Good Values Attract Good People — character, values as magnets, Lewis Alcindor recruitment story
- Chapter 5: Use the Most Powerful Four-Letter Word — love as leadership tool, treating people as family, apartness
- Chapter 6: Call Yourself a Teacher — leader as teacher, patience, demonstration, laws of learning
- Chapter 7: Emotion Is Your Enemy — intensity vs emotionalism, consistency, self-control stories
- Chapter 8: It Takes 10 Hands to Score a Basket — teamwork, sharing credit, managing egos
- Chapter 9: Little Things Make Big Things Happen — details, socks, rivets on a wing
- Chapter 10: Make Each Day Your Masterpiece — time management, 3x5 cards, practice planning
- Chapter 11: The Carrot Is Mightier Than a Stick — motivation through pride not fear, suggestions vs rules
- Chapter 12: Make Greatness Attainable by All — personal greatness, no retiring numbers, hidden potential
- Chapter 13: Seek Significant Change — 1962 breakthrough, the Press, removing self-imposed limitations
- Chapter 14: Don't Look at the Scoreboard — process over outcomes, sealed predictions, Broadway metaphor
- Chapter 15: Adversity Is Your Asset — fate, resilience, Minnesota blizzard story, father's example

### Part 3: Lessons from My Notebook
- Practice schedules, rules, awards, notebook excerpts, coaching methods lists

## Key Frameworks
1. **Pyramid of Success** — 15 building blocks in 5 tiers
2. **Definition of Success** — "Peace of mind from self-satisfaction in knowing you made the effort to become the best of which you are capable"
3. **Four P's** — Planning, Preparation, Practice, Performance
4. **Laws of Learning** — Explanation, Demonstration, Imitation, Correction, Repetition
5. **Two Sets of Three** (from Joshua Wooden) — Never lie/cheat/steal; Don't whine/complain/make excuses
6. **Seven Point Creed** (from Joshua Wooden)

## Core Themes
1. Success = effort, not outcomes
2. Character and values precede talent
3. Leadership through personal example
4. Teaching as the primary function of leadership
5. Emotional discipline and consistency
6. Team-first philosophy (no individual stars)
7. Attention to detail as competitive advantage
8. Time management as leadership skill
9. Love as the foundation of effective leadership
10. Continuous improvement and openness to change
11. Balance and moderation in all things
12. Adversity as a strengthening force
