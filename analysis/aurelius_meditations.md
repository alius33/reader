# Meditations — Marcus Aurelius
## Central Thesis
A Roman Emperor's private journal of Stoic self-examination. Never intended for publication. The core message: you cannot control external events, only your response to them. Virtue is the only true good. Fame, wealth, and pleasure are indifferent. Death is natural. The present moment is all you have. Focus on what is within your power and let go of everything else.
## Key Frameworks: The Dichotomy of Control (what's up to us vs what isn't), Memento Mori (remember you will die), Amor Fati (love your fate), The View from Above (cosmic perspective), Premeditatio Malorum (negative visualisation)
## Best Themes: impermanence of all things, duty and service, anger as self-harm, the obstacle is the way, morning preparation for difficult people
