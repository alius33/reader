# Saudi Arabia's Trump Card Against Iran

## Core Question

What is the third and final force driving conflict in the Middle East, and how did Saudi Arabia — once Iran's close ally — become its bitterest rival and the key player pushing the United States toward war with Iran?

Prof. Jiang frames this lecture as the completion of a three-force model:
- **Force 1 (Lecture 2):** The Israel lobby and Christian Zionism — theological pressure
- **Force 2 (Lecture 3):** Empire economics and financialisation — structural-economic pressure
- **Force 3 (This lecture):** The Saudi-Iran rivalry — geopolitical ally pressure

The central argument: most people think Iran's main enemy is Israel, but in fact Iran's main enemy is Saudi Arabia. Understanding why requires tracing the 1979 earthquake that transformed the entire Middle East.

## Lecture Type

Geopolitical analysis — historical-structural argument. The lecture traces a causal chain from the 1979 Islamic Revolution through the Sunni-Shia religious split, economic competition over oil, three proxy wars (Iraq, Syria, Yemen), Saudi Arabia's vulnerabilities, MBS's alliance with Trump, the assassination of Qasem Soleimani, and the conclusion that Saudi Arabia is actively pushing the United States toward war with Iran because it cannot defeat Iran alone.

## Argument Structure

*Economic dimension:*

**Verdict:** Prof. Jiang uses the three-dimensional model to explain why the rivalry is so intractable — it cannot be resolved by addressing only one dimension.
