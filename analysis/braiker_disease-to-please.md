# Analysis: The Disease to Please — Harriet B. Braiker

## Metadata
- **Author:** Harriet B. Braiker, PhD
- **Year:** 2001
- **Category:** Awareness & Protection
- **Type:** CBT manual with 21-day action plan
- **Core thesis:** People-pleasing is a compulsive, self-defeating syndrome driven by three interconnected forces — distorted thinking (mindsets), habitual behavior (habits), and emotional avoidance (feelings) — change any one to break the cycle

## The Disease to Please Triangle
- Three sides: **Mindsets** (thoughts) + **Habits** (behaviors) + **Feelings** (emotions)
- Each side is both cause AND consequence of the others
- Change in any one side generates change in the whole system
- Dominant type determines entry point for change

### Three Types of People-Pleasers
1. **Cognitive** — driven by need for everyone to like them, define self by what they do for others
2. **Behavioral** — habitual over-giving, can't say no, can't delegate, approval-addicted
3. **Emotionally Avoidant** — fear of anger/conflict/confrontation drives compliance

## Key Concepts

### People-Pleasing Mindsets
- **Ten Commandments of People-Pleasing** — rigid "should" rules that demand compliance
- **Seven Deadly Shoulds** — expectations of what others should give back
- **Niceness as Armor** — belief that being nice protects from rejection/hurt
- **Superstitious Thinking** — "if I'm nice enough, bad things won't happen"
- **Primacy of Others** — world of powerful, demanding others whose needs must come first
- **Enlightened Self-Interest** — taking care of yourself IS taking care of others (not selfish)
- **Approval Addiction** — excessive need for universal approval, rooted in conditional love

### People-Pleasing Habits
- **Conditional Love Origins** — doing good = being good = deserving love
- **Adult Children of Alcoholics** — people-pleasing as survival strategy
- **Childhood Rejection** — compensatory niceness for perceived flaws
- **Parental Expectations** — still living to fulfill parents' vision
- **Love at All Costs** — fostering dependency to prevent abandonment
- **Controlling Men and People-Pleasing Women** — dangerous dynamic
- **Identity Erosion** — gradually losing self to please partner

### People-Pleasing Feelings
- **Fear of Anger** — anger seen as two-gear (on/off), never learned healthy expression
- **"Can Anger Kill?"** — belief that your anger harms others (Arlene's father case)
- **White Door Avoidance Cycle** — escalating avoidance of conflict
- **Words Can Hurt** — verbal abuse scars deeper than physical; teasing IS hostile
- **Mixed Messages** — "angry love," abuse cycle conflates love with pain
- **Healthy Anger Expression** — firm, direct, clear; not explosive or suppressed

## Key Clinical Cases
- **Susan** — weight problem since childhood, people-pleasing to preempt fat-rejection
- **Sarah** — homemaker/mother of four, illness revealed family's ingratitude, went "on strike"
- **Miranda** — always single, smothers men with niceness, drives them away
- **Jennifer/Ron** — made husband so dependent he lost interest, left for another woman
- **Helene/Bob** — powerful CEO submitting to younger man's verbal abuse behind closed doors
- **Gail/Bruce** — film director sculpting partner's appearance, identity erosion, breast implants
- **Dina/Paul** — sexual exploitation escalating from threesomes to pornography, near-destruction
- **Bill/Connie** — man's fear of anger nearly costs him relationship
- **Arlene/Gary** — panic attack from partner's anger, father's death, "anger can kill" belief
- **Molly** — verbal abuse in family, "dinner wasn't over until someone cried," first-strike hostility
- **Samantha** — military family, multiple moves, people-pleasing as social entry strategy

## Distinguishing Features
- **CBT Framework** — thoughts → feelings → behaviors triangle, change one to change all
- **21-Day Structured Program** — specific daily exercises, scripts, techniques
- **Gender Awareness** — extensive coverage of women with controlling men
- **Approval Addiction Concept** — treats people-pleasing as genuine addiction
- **Anger Education** — detailed treatment of healthy vs. unhealthy anger expression
- **"Blowing Off Steam" Myth** — debunks popular belief that explosive anger is cathartic

## Verdict Assessment
- Strengths: Systematic CBT approach, diagnostic quiz, practical 21-day plan, strong on anger education
- Weaknesses: Some case studies extreme (Dina/Paul); repetitive in places; dated references (Oprah, websites)
- Audience: People-pleasers ready for structured self-help with daily practice
- Comparison: More structured than Forward (specific daily plan), less spiritual than Cloud/Townsend, more clinical than Gazipura
