# Man's Search for Meaning — Viktor E. Frankl
## Central Thesis
Those who have a WHY to live can bear almost any HOW. Frankl, a psychiatrist who survived Auschwitz, argues that the primary human drive is not pleasure (Freud) or power (Adler) but meaning. His therapeutic approach, logotherapy, holds that we can find meaning in three ways: through work/creation, through experience/love, and through the attitude we take toward unavoidable suffering.
## Key Frameworks
- Logotherapy (logos = meaning; therapy through finding meaning)
- Three Sources of Meaning: creative values (what we give to the world), experiential values (what we take from the world), attitudinal values (the stance we take toward suffering)
- The Existential Vacuum (modern epidemic of meaninglessness)
- Paradoxical Intention (prescribing the symptom to break anxiety loops)
## Best Stories: Frankl choosing not to escape Auschwitz to stay with his patients; the prisoner who dreamed of liberation on March 30 and died on March 31 when it didn't happen; Frankl's manuscript sewn into his coat lining and lost
