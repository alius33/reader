# No Bad Kids — Janet Lansbury
## Classification & Context
Practical guide / essay collection. Lansbury is a student of Magda Gerber and the RIE (Resources for Infant Educarers) approach. The book is a curated collection of 32 articles from her popular website JanetLansbury.com, organized around toddler discipline. Each chapter is a self-contained essay addressing a specific topic. The core audience is parents of toddlers (1-3), though principles extend to older children. Written in an accessible, conversational tone with real parent letters and responses throughout.
## Central Thesis
There are no bad kids — only impressionable young people wrestling with emotions and impulses, trying to communicate their needs the only way they know how. The key to toddler discipline is confident, calm leadership: setting clear, firm boundaries without anger, shame, punishment, or emotional drama. The parent should be like a "benevolent CEO" — nonchalant, matter-of-fact, unruffled. Children NEED limits (lack of discipline is neglect), but limits should be delivered with empathy, honesty, and respect. The emotional state of the parent almost always dictates the child's reaction.
## Key Frameworks
- RIE Philosophy: babies and toddlers are whole people — sentient, aware, intuitive, communicative. Treat them with respect from birth.
- The Benevolent CEO: respond to misbehaviour like a calm, confident leader — not with anger, lectures, or emotional reactions
- "Staying Unruffled": the parent's emotional state sets the tone. Calm authority invites cooperation; anxiety/anger invites escalation.
- Eight Reasons Toddlers Push Limits: SOS/fatigue, need for clarity, over-reaction created drama, testing leadership, releasing feelings, imitating parents, seeking attention, needing love reassurance
- "All Feelings Allowed, Not All Behaviour": children may express any emotion; certain behaviours (hitting, throwing, etc.) are stopped calmly and without shame
- The Healing Power of Tantrums: tantrums are healthy emotional release, not problems to fix. Stay present, don't punish the feelings.
- No Time-Outs, No Punishments, No Bribes, No Distractions: natural consequences only; honest, direct communication
- Sportscasting: narrating what you see without judgment — "You're trying to take that toy. I won't let you grab it."
## Best Stories
1. Lansbury's son hitting at RIE class when overtired — she recognises the SOS and takes him home, acknowledging her own failure to leave sooner
2. Her four-year-old daughter being rude to Grandma after seven hours of travel — Lansbury intervenes calmly, carries her out screaming, then realises it was exhaustion
3. Multiple parent letters and responses throughout showing real-world application of RIE principles
4. Magda Gerber asking in her Hungarian accent: "Time out of what? Time out of life?"
5. The "gentle discipline in action" chapter — a full narrative of a parent implementing every principle in a single challenging day
