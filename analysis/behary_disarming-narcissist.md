# Disarming the Narcissist — Wendy Behary

## Classification & Context

- **Category:** Awareness & Protection — surviving and thriving with narcissistic individuals
- **Discipline:** Clinical psychology, schema therapy, interpersonal neurobiology
- **Audience:** Partners, family members, colleagues, and therapists dealing with narcissistic individuals
- **Approach:** Empathy-based confrontation rather than "run away" — unique in the narcissism literature
- **Edition:** Second edition (2013), expanded from 2008 original with new material on female narcissists and a chapter on escaping dangerous narcissism
- **Forewords by:** Jeffrey Young (founder of schema therapy) and Daniel J. Siegel (interpersonal neurobiology pioneer)

## Central Thesis

The narcissist's abrasive, entitled, and empathy-deficient behaviour is rooted in childhood emotional deprivation, conditional love, and shame — not inherent malice. By understanding your own schemas (life traps) and the narcissist's wounded inner child, you can use **empathic confrontation** — simultaneously extending understanding while holding firm boundaries — to survive, assert yourself, and potentially influence change. This is not about tolerating abuse; it's about strategically combining empathy with accountability and leverage to break destructive relational patterns.

### Chapter 1: Framing the Picture
- **Diagnostic checklist** (13 traits): egocentric, entitled, demeaning, demanding, distrustful, perfectionist, snobbish, approval-seeking, unempathetic, remorseless, compulsive, addictive, emotionally detached
- **Three origin stories:**
  1. The Spoiled Child — no limits, no consequences, taught superiority
  2. The Dependent Child — everything done for them, robbed of competence
  3. The Lonely/Deprived Child (most common) — conditional love, performance-based affection
- **Combination types:** Spoiled-Dependent, Deprived-Dependent (the "high-maintenance" type)
- **Four masks/coping modes:** The Bully, The Show-Off, The Entitled One, The Addictive Self-Soother
- **Covert narcissism:** the morally righteous martyr disguise
- **Female narcissists ("Narcissisters"):** vanity-focused, victim/martyr role, drama queens; narcissistic mothers as particularly high-stakes
- **Healthy narcissism:** exists on a spectrum; healthy adult narcissists are empathic, engaging, driven, confrontational without soul-destroying

### Chapter 2: Understanding the Anatomy of Personality
- **Cognitive therapy** (Aaron Beck): examining biased meaning-making
- **Schema therapy** (Jeffrey Young): 18 Early Maladaptive Schemas
  - Full list with descriptions of all 18 schemas
  - Schemas triggered IN YOU by narcissists: self-sacrifice, subjugation, abandonment, defectiveness, emotional inhibition, emotional deprivation, mistrust, unrelenting standards
  - Schemas typical OF narcissists: emotional deprivation, mistrust, defectiveness/shame, subjugation, unrelenting standards, entitlement/grandiosity, insufficient self-control, approval-seeking
- **Interpersonal neurobiology** (Daniel Siegel): attachment, memory (implicit vs. explicit), the brain's file-folder system
- **Louis case study** introduced — 58-year-old retired narcissist whose wife Francine gives an ultimatum
- **Key insight:** the narcissist's coping masks (perfectionist, vindictive bully, competitive braggart) all compensate for deep shame

### Chapter 3: Getting Hooked
- **Three schema clusters in narcissist-partners:**
  1. Mistrust + Subjugation
  2. Defectiveness + Unrelenting Standards
  3. Abandonment + Emotional Deprivation + Self-Sacrifice
- **Extended case studies:** Bill (the trainer), Beth (the assistant), Wendy (the therapist herself) — all triggered by Louis
- **The burden, not the blame:** exercise differentiating fault from responsibility
- **"Feeling felt"** (Siegel): contingent communication where signals are actually received and reflected back
- **Leverage and incentive:** what creates openings for change — significant potential loss, health crisis, retirement, loneliness

### Chapter 4: Getting Past the Obstacles
- **Four Stages of Transformation:** Observe → Assess → Identify → Differentiate
- **Five "bait and switch" manoeuvres** narcissists use: The Disappearing Act, The Setup, Dr. Jekyll/Mr. Hyde, Adding Insult to Injury, Devil's Advocate
- **Typical emotional responses:** insecurity, intimidation, resentment, provocation, helplessness
- **The "low road"** (Siegel): when subcortical threat detection hijacks prefrontal executive function
- **Fight/Flight/Freeze → Counter-attack/Avoidance/Surrender** — specific modifications and communication scripts for each
- **Sensory system awareness:** physical signals when schemas activate (heart rate, temperature, throat tightness, etc.)

### Chapter 5: Paying Attention
- **Mindfulness practice:** detailed 5-minute twice-daily exercise engaging all senses
- **"That was then and this is now"** mantra for differentiating schema-driven reactions
- **Strategies for the 4 masks:**
  - Show-Off → offer modest praise for ordinary gestures, ignore grandiose bids
  - Bully → eye contact, calm articulation of impact, firm boundary
  - Entitled One → call aside, state impact, refuse to apologize for their behaviour
  - Addictive Self-Soother → acknowledge the hiding, express care and concern, propose professional help

### Chapter 6: Making an Exit
- **Dangerous narcissism indicators:** financial/legal threats, physical/emotional safety threats, relationship/community stability threats
- **Sexual malfeasance focus:** pornography addiction, infidelity, prostitutes, chat rooms — brain hijacking by sexual stimulation
- **Samantha and Todd case study:** escalation from denial → rage → threats → physical intimidation
- **Deciding to stay or go:** safety first, especially with escalating volatility
- **Reforming the moderate narcissist:** three requirements — feeling understood, expressing needs safely, recognising changes
- **Impact on children:** they internalize narcissistic or submissive patterns

### Chapter 7: Using Empathic Confrontation (THE CORE CHAPTER)
- **Empathy vs. Compassion distinction:**
  - Empathy = understanding/feeling the other's experience (doesn't require agreement)
  - Compassion = empathy + desire to comfort/relieve suffering (goes further)
- **Neuroscience of empathy:** mirror neurons, fMRI evidence, context-dependent access
- **Sue and Don case study:** wife's empathic attunement to husband's father's death despite his stonewalling
- **"Limited reparenting" technique** (from Young): nurturing the lonely/deprived child within the narcissist
- **Photo technique:** keep a childhood photo of the narcissist (and yourself) to build empathy
- **Five empathic confrontation scenarios:**
  1. Differentiating fault and responsibility (Steven/Sharon — late pickup)
  2. Setting limits (mother-in-law rearranging nursery)
  3. Establishing rules of reciprocity (Chris — restaurant choice)
  4. Promoting awareness via positive feedback (Rick — phone call)
  5. Integrating tools: raw truth with compassion (Ed/Karen — workout conflict)
- **Three leverage tools:**
  1. Implicit Assumption Rule (benefit of the doubt)
  2. Micro-to-Macro approach (dress rehearsal — pointing out how behaviour affects wider world)
  3. Time-outs (breathe, schema flash cards, healthy distractions)

### Chapter 8: Seven Communication Gifts
- **FORCE framework:** Flexibility, Openness, Receptivity, Competency, Enlightenment
- **Seven gifts/arts:**
  1. Mutual Respect → Gift of Generosity
  2. Self-Disclosure → Gift of Courage
  3. Discernment → Gift of Truth
  4. Collaboration → Gift of Shared Effort
  5. Anticipating Confrontations → Gift of Foresight
  6. Apology → Gift of Responsibility

## Key Principles (15)

1. Narcissism originates from unmet childhood needs, not inherent evil
2. The narcissist's behaviour is not your fault, but your response IS your responsibility
3. Empathy is understanding, not agreeing or tolerating
4. Leverage (meaningful consequences) is essential for any narcissistic change
5. Your own schemas are the hooks that keep you trapped in the pattern
6. Mindfulness creates the gap between trigger and response
7. "That was then and this is now" — differentiate past-driven reactions from present reality
8. Confrontation without empathy is attack; empathy without confrontation is enabling
9. The narcissist's inner child is terrified, ashamed, and lonely — see that child behind the mask
10. Schemas operate below conscious awareness via implicit memory
11. The brain can change (neuroplasticity), therefore personality is flexible
12. Reciprocity is non-negotiable in any healthy relationship
13. Not all narcissists are dangerous, but dangerous ones require exit strategies, not empathy experiments
14. Model what you expect — communication, accountability, vulnerability
15. Change is possible but requires persistent effort, alavancagem, and professional help
