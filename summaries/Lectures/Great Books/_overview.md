# Great Books — Series Overview

Prof. Jiang Xueqin's 8-lecture series on the foundational texts of Western civilisation.

## Lectures

| # | Title | Status |
|---|-------|--------|
| 01 | Secrets of the Universe | Summarised — The universe is conscious, not material; great books are portals to immortality, reincarnation, and Godhood |
| 02 | Homer and the Invention of the Human | Summarised — Harold Bloom's consciousness criterion, three-layer speech analysis of Agamemnon/Achilles, brain-as-antenna theory, poets = prophets = teachers |
| 03 | Poets and Prophets | Summarised — One poem creates a civilisation; arete and eudaimonia frame Greek life, Kant's filter theory explains language, and poets are the unacknowledged legislators of the world |
| 04 | The Conscious Universe | Summarised — The universe is conscious and we are holograms of it; love unifies and imagination animates; great books compress many lives of empathy into one reading |
| 05 | The Odyssey | Summarised — A family story of three traumatised souls; Odysseus's worldview of justice, family, and legacy collapses at Troy, producing PTSD that the Odyssey must repair |
| 06 | The Intimacy of Love | Summarised — Love is intimacy, not obsession; the Odyssey's climax is the alignment of mind, spirit, and soul through a secret language only lovers share |
| 07 | The Anti-Homer | Summarised — Virgil's *Aeneid* is a deliberate inversion of Homer commissioned by Augustus; love becomes evil, forgiveness becomes folly, and the seed of a thousand-year Dark Age is planted |
| 08 | The Poetry of Empire | Summarised — The Aeneid as political propaganda: Virgil inverts Homer by replacing love with piety, stripping Aeneas of humanity until he can kill without divine prompting; Dante will be the cure |
