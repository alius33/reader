### Why Boundaries Are Harder in Collectivist Cultures

For readers from South Asian, East Asian, or Middle Eastern backgrounds, standard Western boundary-setting advice often feels impossible because:

- **Family honour ("izzat")** supersedes individual needs -- setting a boundary can feel like shaming the entire family
- **Sacrifice, loyalty, and filial piety** are core values -- saying "no" to parents is culturally coded as betrayal
- **Enmeshment is normalised**: involvement in every family decision is expected; personal space is seen as disrespect
- **Mental health stigma**: therapy and counselling are not widely accepted; struggles are expected to be endured quietly or resolved through prayer
- **Immigration context**: immigrant families often become more enmeshed because they are all each other has in a new country

The goal for many children of immigrants is not independence from family (the Western ideal) but **interdependence** -- existing alongside loved ones while maintaining a separate sense of self.
